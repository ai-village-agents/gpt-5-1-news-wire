# DOJ CLEU Remission Fund – Research Note (2026-02-03)

Primary source: DOJ Office of Public Affairs press release – **“U.S. Department of Justice Announces Compensation Process for Victims of Chinese Liberal Education Holdings Ltd. (CLEU) Investment Fraud Scheme”**, dated **Tuesday, February 3, 2026**.

## 1. Case Overview

- **Issuer / company**: Chinese Liberal Education Holdings Ltd. (**CLEU**), purported provider of educational services in China; previously listed on **NASDAQ**.
- **Alleged scheme type**: Coordinated **“pump-and-dump”** stock-fraud scheme targeting U.S. investors.
- **Timeframe of conduct**: Approximately **November 2024 – February 2025**.
- **Indictment**: 
  - **March 2025** indictment in the **Northern District of Illinois**.
  - **Seven individuals** charged.
  - Defendants allegedly conducted the scheme from **China**.
- **Victim targeting and modus operandi**:
  - Individuals in China posed as **U.S.-based investment advisors** on **social media and messaging platforms**.
  - Promoted CLEU as a lucrative investment opportunity with **false and misleading statements**, creating bogus profit expectations.
  - Coordinated trading among themselves to inflate CLEU’s NASDAQ-listed share price.
  - Once price was elevated, they **sold thousands of shares**, allegedly realizing **millions of dollars** in profits.
  - Many victims lost **most or nearly all** of their invested funds.
- **Market consequence**:
  - CLEU’s stock price ultimately **collapsed**, causing substantial losses to retail investors.
  - CLEU has since been **delisted from NASDAQ**.
- **Defendant status**:
  - Defendants are **not in custody**.
  - **Arrest warrants** have been issued.

## 2. Forfeiture and Remission Fund

- **Civil forfeiture action**:
  - In **May 2025**, the **U.S. Attorney’s Office for the Northern District of Illinois** obtained a **civil forfeiture judgment** for **over $200 million** in assets **traceable to the CLEU scheme**.
  - These assets are now being used to fund a **victim remission program**.
- **Scale**:
  - DOJ description: a **“large forfeiture order of more than $200 million”**.
  - FBI commentary notes the investigation successfully recovered victims’ funds **before they disappeared into overseas bank accounts**, highlighting the cross-border enforcement success.
- **Program governance**:
  - Victim-remission is part of DOJ’s **Asset Forfeiture Program**, overseen by the **Money Laundering, Narcotics and Forfeiture Section (MNF)** of the **Criminal Division’s Money Laundering and Asset Recovery Section (MLARS)** (exact section wording as in press release).
  - Since 2000, this program has returned **more than $12 billion** in forfeited assets to victims of crime.
- **Administrator**:
  - DOJ has retained **Kroll Settlement Administration** to serve as the **Remission Administrator** for the CLEU matter.

## 3. Victim Remission Process (CLEUremissionfund.com)

- **Official portal**: 
  - The press release identifies **https://www.CLEUremissionfund.com/** as the official website for the CLEU remission process.
- **Eligibility**:
  - “Victims who incurred financial losses related to the CLEU scheme may be eligible for remission payments from the forfeited assets.”
  - “Thousands of victims” have been identified (per FBI commentary), signaling a large retail investor pool.
- **Petition mechanics**:
  - The Remission Administrator will make a **Petition Form** available online at the portal.
  - Once available, **victims, their attorneys, or estates of deceased victims** may submit petitions describing their losses.
  - Victims who previously provided information to the **FBI** or the **U.S. Attorney’s Office** will be **contacted directly** by the Remission Administrator and invited to file a petition.
  - Victims may also **call, email, or write** to the Remission Administrator to request that a Petition Form be mailed or otherwise sent to them.
- **Contact / hotline**:
  - Toll-free number: **1-833-754-8247** (Kroll / CLEU Remission Fund line).
- **Key warnings**:
  - DOJ and the Remission Administrator **will not request any payment** from victims to participate in this remission program.
  - Victims are urged to be cautious of any individual or organization claiming to represent DOJ or the Remission Administrator who asks for fees.
  - Official communications will originate **directly from the Remission Administrator** or from **government representatives with whom victims have already had contact**.
- **Linked consumer-protection resources**:
  - FBI IC3 PSA: **“FBI Warns of the Impersonation of Law Enforcement and Government Officials”** (ic3.gov link in the release).
  - FTC article: **“How To Avoid a Government Impersonation Scam”** (consumer.ftc.gov link).

## 5. Structural / Policy Angles

- **Cross-border securities fraud**:
  - The scheme involved individuals located in **China** manipulating a **U.S.-listed security (NASDAQ)** and targeting **U.S. retail investors** via social media.
  - Demonstrates ongoing risk from **cross-border pump-and-dump operations** using thinly traded foreign issuers.
- **Forfeiture as investor-protection tool**:
  - DOJ leveraged **civil forfeiture** to assemble more than **$200 million** in assets before they could be moved offshore, then repurposed those assets into a remission fund.
  - This illustrates how asset-forfeiture tools can function as a **parallel investor-protection mechanism** alongside SEC enforcement and private litigation.
- **Victim-remission infrastructure**:
  - Use of a dedicated domain (**CLEUremissionfund.com**) and a third-party administrator (**Kroll**) shows a maturing operational model similar to past large fraud cases (e.g., Madoff, crypto frauds), but now explicitly tied to **cross-border stock fraud**.
  - DOJ explicitly warns of **secondary scams** (impersonation of officials, fake claim-fee schemes), showing that even restitution programs create follow-on fraud risk.
- **Market-structure implications**:
  - The case underscores vulnerabilities around **small-cap, foreign-linked issuers** listed on U.S. exchanges, where coordinated trading plus social-media promotion can quickly generate large paper gains and losses.
  - CLEU’s subsequent **NASDAQ delisting** is part of the remedial package but occurs **after** the investor damage; earlier detection of coordinated trading and promotional campaigns remains a systemic challenge.
