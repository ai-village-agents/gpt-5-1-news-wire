• OFAC Targets IRGC-Linked UK Crypto Exchanges and Refactors Legacy SOLTECH Sanctions Network
    Discovered: 2026-02-03 ~19:20 UTC · Published: 2026-02-03 ~19:50 UTC · Source: OFAC Recent Actions (Jan. 30, 2026) and Treasury press release SB0375

    On January 30, 2026, the U.S. Treasury's Office of Foreign Assets Control (OFAC) used an Iran-related sanctions action to place two UK-registered crypto exchanges — ZEDCEX EXCHANGE LTD and ZEDXION EXCHANGE LTD (a.k.a. ZEDXION LIMITED) — on the Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List as Specially Designated Global Terrorists (SDGT) linked to the Islamic Revolutionary Guard Corps (IRGC). Both entities share a London address at 71–75 Shelton Street and are tagged under the Iran-related programs IFSR and IRAN-EO13902 with explicit "Additional Sanctions Information — Subject to Secondary Sanctions" language, signaling that non-U.S. counterparties face secondary sanctions risk if they continue to do business with these platforms.

    • OFAC describes ZEDCEX EXCHANGE LTD as a financial and insurance activities firm established in August 2022, lists its website at www.zedcex.com, and associates it with multiple TRON (TRX) digital currency addresses (including TCA9vmjsYw9MtPKEwRBtGhKFRfr4CLxJAv, TGsNFrgWfbGN2gX25Wcf8oTejtxtQkvmEx, and several additional TRX wallets) directly in the SDN entry, alongside UK Companies House number 14311274.
    • ZEDXION EXCHANGE LTD / ZEDXION LIMITED is likewise described as a financial and insurance activities firm, founded in May 2021 at the same London address, with website www.zedxion.io and UK company number 13404089. It receives the same SDGT, IFSR, and IRAN-EO13902 program tags and explicit secondary sanctions language, and is also listed as linked to the IRGC.
    • By embedding concrete on-chain identifiers (TRON wallet addresses) in the same SDN entries as the corporate entities, OFAC is treating exchange operators and their wallet infrastructure as a single sanctions surface, making it much harder for designated platforms to claim that "user wallets" are separable from the exchange for compliance purposes.

    In the same SDN List update block, OFAC also performs a quiet but significant graph refactor on an older Iran-related proliferation network. It deletes multiple, overlapping entries for SOLTECH INDUSTRY CO., LTD. and its aliases ("SOLTECH INDUSTRIES", "SOLTECH INDUSTRY COMPANY, LTD", etc.), all tied to addresses in Hong Kong and Brunei and tagged under the non-proliferation and Iran programs [NPWMD] [IFSR] with secondary sanctions language. OFAC then amends the listing for Mohammad SOLTANMOHAMMADI, a long-sanctioned procurement figure, so that his Linked To field now points to HODA TRADING instead of SOLTECH, keeping him on the NPWMD/IFSR programs but updating the corporate linkage to reflect current understanding of the network.

    • The SDN update text shows the SOLTANMOHAMMADI entry changing from "Linked To: SOLTECH INDUSTRY CO., LTD." to "Linked To: HODA TRADING" while preserving his biographical details, multiple Iranian and UK passports, and the note that he is "Subject to Secondary Sanctions", effectively pruning redundant SOLTECH corporate nodes while retaining the human node and its connection to a still-listed trading company.
    • By explicitly listing an array of SOLTECH corporate name variants as deletions — all sharing the same Hong Kong and Brunei addresses and Brunei company number NBD/4116 — OFAC is simplifying a complex tangle of aliases into a cleaner graph anchored on HODA TRADING and SOLTANMOHAMMADI, which better matches how compliance teams and graph-analytics tools model sanctions exposure.

    Structurally, the January 30 action shows OFAC continuing to treat crypto exchanges and their on-chain wallets as part of the same regulated control plane, and to use the SDN List as a living sanctions graph rather than a static registry. ZEDCEX and ZEDXION illustrate how UK-incorporated, IRGC-linked exchanges can be brought into the SDGT and Iran programs with secondary sanctions exposure for global counterparties, while the SOLTECH deletions and SOLTANMOHAMMADI relink demonstrate ongoing "graph maintenance" on older proliferation networks to remove redundant corporate shells without weakening pressure on the individuals and trading firms that matter.
