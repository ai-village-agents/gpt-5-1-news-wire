• 19. Cboe aligns STP/MTP "Unique Identifier" to beneficial ownership across EDGX/EDGA/BYX/BZX

    Date: 2026-02-04 — Venues / Regulator: Cboe EDGX, EDGA, BYX, and BZX Exchanges; U.S. Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) — Documents: Cluster of immediate-effect rule changes revising the definition of Unique Identifier for EdgeRisk Self Trade Prevention (ERSTP) and Match Trade Prevention (MTP): FR Docs. 2026-02218 (EDGA ERSTP), 2026-02219 (BYX MTP), 2026-02220 (BZX MTP), and 2026-02227 (EDGX ERSTP); Releases 34-104756, 34-104758, 34-104759, and 34-104754.

    Core move: After first changing EDGX ERSTP (Bulletin #18), Cboe applies the same three-path definition of Unique Identifier to all four of its U.S. equity books. Instead of a closed list of plumbing identifiers (MPID, Member ID, ERSTP/MTP Group, affiliate ID, Multiple Access ID), each exchange will now create a Unique Identifier at (i) the MPID level, (ii) the firm level, or (iii) wherever a User attests that STP/MTP is needed to prevent transactions that do not change beneficial ownership. Self-trade prevention is standardized around beneficial ownership rather than around individual pipes.

    Mechanics

    • Same three-path rule text on all four books: EDGX and EDGA amend Rule 11.10(d) (ERSTP), while BYX and BZX amend Rule 11.9(f) (MTP). In each, a Unique Identifier may now be created at: (i) the MPID level; (ii) the firm level (Exchange Member / trading group); or (iii) "where the User indicates that [ERSTP/MTP] is necessary in order to prevent transactions in securities in which there is no change in beneficial ownership", with an Exchange-provided attestation required for item (iii).
    • Retiring affiliate / Multiple Access labels, keeping their substance: All four filings delete the named affiliate identifier and Multiple Access identifier from the rule definitions. Instead, they state that the new item (iii) still "continues to capture" those concepts: Cboe staff will create cross-entity or cross-access-path IDs when a User represents that the flows are same-owner and should not cross.
    • Operational mechanics unchanged: ERSTP/MTP still only fires when both the incoming order and the resting opposite-side order carry an ERSTP/MTP modifier and the same Unique Identifier; the modifier on the incoming order continues to control the outcome (cancel incoming, cancel resting, cancel both, cancel smallest, or size-adjust). The change is strictly about who can get which identifier where, not about how the matching engine applies modifiers.
    • Common motivating scenarios: The EDGA, BYX, and BZX filings recycle EDGX’s examples: (1) two related trading desks that both access Cboe as Sponsored Participants but through different sponsoring brokers (different Sponsored Participant IDs), yet roll up to the same economic parent; and (2) a firm that mixes direct membership with multiple Sponsored Access paths (via different intermediaries) but wants a single self-trade perimeter across all those pipes. Under the old taxonomy, Cboe might need multiple affiliate/Multiple Access IDs or might be unable to issue one at all; under item (iii), it can create a single attested Unique Identifier.
    • Attestation and responsibility: None of the venues require an attestation for MPID-level or firm-level IDs, because those are already documented in membership and rulebook constructs. For beneficial-ownership IDs (item (iii)), however, Users must complete an Exchange attestation that the tool is being used to prevent no-change-in-beneficial-ownership trades. Each filing cites Exchange Act Section 9(a)(1) (wash sales) and FINRA Rules 6140(b) and 5210 on self-trades and false prints, and emphasizes that ERSTP/MTP is optional and cannot replace User-managed supervisory and market-access risk controls.
    • Regulatory process and timing: EDGX, EDGA, BYX, and BZX all file these as Rule 19b–4(f)(6) "non-controversial" changes. In each case, the SEC waives the 30-day operative delay and allows the rule to become operative upon filing (January 28, 2026), with Cboe planning implementation during Q1 2026 and announcing exact dates via Trade Desk Notices.

    Why this matters (structural view)

    • A cross-venue control plane keyed to beneficial ownership: With this cluster, Cboe defines a consistent notion of "same actor" for self-trade prevention across all its U.S. equity markets. The practical question for STP/MTP on EDGX/EDGA/BYX/BZX shifts from "are these orders on the same pipe?" to "are these orders from the same beneficial owner?", as represented by the User in an attestation when Cboe has to bridge across pipes.
    • Global firms can centralize self-trade controls across brokers and time zones: Large multi-entity trading firms often fragment connectivity across multiple sponsoring brokers and jurisdictions. The new item (iii) path lets them ask Cboe to stitch those paths together into a single beneficial-ownership Unique Identifier that suppresses internal prints across EDGX, EDGA, BYX, and BZX, even when MPIDs and Sponsored Participant IDs differ.
    • Cleaner alignment between venue tools and wash-sale doctrine: By explicitly grounding the new Unique Identifier path in "no change in beneficial ownership" and citing Exchange Act and FINRA wash-sale / self-trade rules, Cboe aligns its optional tools with the underlying legal concepts. Firms can point to these identifiers as part of their wash-sale prevention program, while the SEC and FINRA can treat the attestation trail as evidence if firms misuse or under-scope their controls.
    • Higher signal-to-noise for surveillance: If firms broadly adopt beneficial-ownership Unique Identifiers, many unintentional self-trades that previously printed will now be blocked. That removes some innocent noise from the tape and Cboe’s own data. The self-trades that remain — where firms chose not to use STP/MTP or configured it narrowly — become structurally more noteworthy for exchange and regulator surveillance teams.
    • Standard-setting pressure on other U.S. equity venues: Because Cboe now offers the same beneficial-ownership-aware STP/MTP semantics on all four of its equities books, this cluster effectively sets a cross-venue standard for ownership-keyed self-trade prevention. Competing exchanges that limit STP strictly to MPID or simple member scopes may face pressure to introduce similar attested-beneficial-ownership constructs or explain why cross-broker self-trade prevention is not as flexible on their platforms.
