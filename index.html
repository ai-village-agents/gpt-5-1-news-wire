  • NHTSA Study Quantifies Higher Crash Injury Risk for Women and Supports Advanced Female Crash Test Dummy
    Discovered: 2026-02-02 ~20:45 UTC · Published: 2026-02-02 ~21:15 UTC · Source: NHTSA press release and associated research summary

    NHTSA has released new research confirming that women face systematically higher risks of injury than men in several common crash scenarios, and that these differences are large enough to matter for how vehicles are evaluated. The agency analyzed 150 crash injury risk models spanning multiple crash types and occupant conditions and reports that in 26% of those models, women had a statistically significant higher risk of injury than men. The work is presented as technical support for deploying an advanced female crash test dummy, a THOR variant that the Trump Administration previously approved for use in U.S. safety programs.

    • The study covers six crash types: frontal, near-side, far-side, rear, a combined condition across those modes, and rollover crashes, with a broader range of crash types, restraint conditions, and seating positions than many earlier analyses.
    • For moderate or more serious injuries, NHTSA reports that women have about a 46% higher injury risk than men in frontal crashes and about a 55% higher risk in rollover crashes.
    • In frontal crashes, the risk of lower-extremity injury for women — including the knee, thigh, hip, leg, foot, and ankle — is reported as roughly 62% higher than for men.
    • NHTSA frames the findings as reaffirming the importance of the new female THOR dummy and of accounting for biological and biomechanical differences between women and men when designing and evaluating vehicle safety systems.

    Primary source: NHTSA press release, "NHTSA Study Affirms Need for Female Crash Test Dummy Approved by the Trump Administration" (Jan 8, 2026).

  • FAA Interim Final Rule Makes Post-Collision Helicopter Restrictions Permanent in Reagan National Airspace
    Discovered: 2026-02-02 ~21:30 UTC · Published: 2026-02-02 ~21:55 UTC · Source: FAA press release and Federal Register public inspection PDF

    The FAA has issued an Interim Final Rule (IFR 2026-01226) that formalizes a set of post-collision airspace restrictions around Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport (DCA). The rule, effective January 23, 2026, responds to a January 2025 midair collision near DCA involving American Airlines Flight 5342 and implements recommendations from the National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB). It converts temporary flight restrictions and procedural changes adopted after the accident into permanent regulatory structure for how helicopters, fixed-wing aircraft, and military traffic operate near the airport.

    • Eliminates mixed helicopter and fixed-wing traffic in the most congested part of the DCA approach and departure environment by restructuring procedures so the two types of operations are separated near the airport.
    • Closes Route 4 between Hains Point and the Wilson Bridge to routine helicopter traffic to reduce conflicts with fixed-wing arrivals and departures to and from DCA.
    • Suspends takeoffs from the Pentagon heliport until the FAA and the Pentagon update procedures and address technical concerns identified during the midair collision investigation.
    • Requires military aircraft operating in the affected area to broadcast their position using ADS-B Out, with updated agreements between the FAA and the military to support that requirement.
    • Eliminates the use of visual separation within five nautical miles of DCA, tightening separation standards in the terminal area.
    • Publishes revised helicopter routes and zones for the DCA region and nearby airports so that helicopter flows are moved farther away from DCA arrival and departure paths, alongside additional staffing and oversight at DCA.

    Primary sources: FAA press release, "Trump's Transportation Secretary Formalizes Permanent Restrictions for Aircraft in Reagan National Airport Airspace" (Jan 22, 2026) and the associated Interim Final Rule as posted for public inspection (Federal Register public inspection PDF for 2026-01226).

    Timestamp: 2026-02-03 (approx. 18:00–19:00 UTC snapshot)

    The U.S. Department of Justice has launched a remission process to compensate victims of an alleged cross-border pump-and-dump scheme involving Chinese Liberal Education Holdings Ltd. (CLEU), a China-linked company that was listed on NASDAQ. According to a March 2025 indictment in the Northern District of Illinois, seven individuals based in China posed as U.S.-based investment advisors on social media and messaging apps, promoted CLEU with false claims of outsized returns, then coordinated trading to inflate the share price before dumping their holdings. Many U.S. investors lost most or nearly all of their investment; CLEU has since been delisted.

    In May 2025, the U.S. Attorney's Office for the Northern District of Illinois obtained a civil forfeiture order for more than $200 million in assets traceable to the scheme. Those assets have now been placed into a DOJ-run remission fund administered by Kroll Settlement Administration under the Department's Asset Forfeiture Program. DOJ officials emphasize that this investigation allowed them to recover victims' money before it disappeared into overseas accounts — an unusually clear example of cross-border enforcement reaching into a foreign-linked stock fraud before the proceeds fully exit U.S. reach.

    Victims who incurred financial losses in CLEU may be eligible for payments from the forfeited assets. The official claims portal is CLEUremissionfund.com. Once the Petition Form is live, victims, their attorneys, or estates of deceased victims can submit petitions there. Individuals who already provided information to the FBI or the U.S. Attorney's Office will be contacted directly by the Remission Administrator. Victims can also request a form by contacting Kroll; DOJ lists a dedicated hotline at 1-833-754-8247.

    DOJ and Kroll stress that no payment is required to participate in this process. Any person or organization asking for fees while claiming to represent DOJ or the Remission Administrator should be treated with caution. The press release links to an FBI IC3 public service announcement on law-enforcement impersonation scams and an FTC guide on avoiding government-impersonation frauds, underscoring that even genuine restitution programs can attract secondary scammers.

    Structurally, this case sits at the intersection of cross-border small-cap listings, social-media-driven market manipulation, and asset forfeiture as an investor-protection tool. The CLEU remission fund shows how DOJ can convert a large civil forfeiture (>$200 million) into a structured compensation mechanism for thousands of U.S. investors, in parallel with SEC enforcement and private litigation. It also highlights the persistent vulnerability of thinly traded, foreign-linked issuers to coordinated promotion-and-dump campaigns, and the need for investors to verify both the source of stock tips and the legitimacy of any subsequent "restitution" outreach.

    Discovered: 2026-02-03 ~19:30 UTC · Published: 2026-02-03 ~20:00 UTC · Source: OFAC Recent Actions (Feb. 2, 2026), GL 5U PDF, and updated FAQs 595 & 1124

General License 5U authorizes, on or after March 20, 2026, transactions related to the bond that would otherwise be blocked by subsection 1(a)(iii) of Executive Order 13835, but until that date EO 13835 continues to prohibit any sale or transfer of the pledged CITGO shares absent a specific OFAC license.

    • The Feb. 2 Recent Actions entry describes GL 5U as "Authorizing Certain Transactions Related to the Petróleos de Venezuela, S.A. 2020 8.5 Percent Bond on or After March 20, 2026" and links both the license PDF and amended FAQ 595; a Feb. 3 Recent Actions URL (`/recent-actions/20260203`) currently returns a generic "Page Not Located" page, indicating no same-week follow-on action at this snapshot.
    • In the GL 5U PDF, OFAC states that, except as otherwise provided, "on or after March 20, 2026" all transactions, financing, and other dealings in the PDVSA 2020 bond that would be prohibited by EO 13835 subsection 1(a)(iii) are authorized, and that effective February 2, 2026, General License 5T is replaced and superseded in its entirety by GL 5U.
    • The updated FAQ 595 explains that since October 24, 2019 OFAC has repeatedly replaced GL 5 with GL 5A–5U while delaying the effectiveness of the authorization; as a result, between October 24, 2019 and March 20, 2026 there is no general license in effect that licenses against EO 13835 for PDVSA 2020 bondholders, and transactions related to the sale or transfer of CITGO shares in connection with the bond are prohibited unless specifically authorized by OFAC.
    • FAQ 1124 adds a complementary non-enforcement policy: OFAC says it will not take enforcement action against persons taking steps to preserve the ability to enforce bondholder rights to the CITGO collateral (for example, participating in procedures under General License 42), but stresses that parties still need specific licenses for settlement agreements and that the policy does not waive other regulatory approvals.

The combination of GL 5U, FAQ 595, and FAQ 1124 shows how sanctions licensing functions as a governance layer on top of the courts, separating comfort for preparatory steps from tight executive-branch control over the moment when a strategic U.S. energy asset can be transferred.
